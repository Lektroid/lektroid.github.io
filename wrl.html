Slide 3: Planning & Organisation

For this project I was required to take field recordings and create a surround soundscape of water effects for use in theatre. We went out on location to Glenburn Waterfall, North Ayrshire.

First we decided on the project and agreed on a 3D soundscape of a waterfall and the river coming away from it. Then we had to decide on a location, which had to be accessible and safe in order to take a lot of recording equipment. We decided on Glenburn Waterfall since it is local, and close to the parking position. There is safe access, which is located within a small gorge, which has an atmospheric audio quality. There are also several nesting birds which gave a very dynamic impression of the landscape.

It was important to have accessibility behind the waterfall as well as the small rapids running away from the waterfall for total immersivity.

Ambisonic recording front and rear of waterfall. The rear of the waterfall has a cave which results in natural reverberation, this would be advantageous. Recording water disturbance with the hydrophones, placed near and far from the waterfall, using shotgun microphones to enhance sense of detail in specific targeted locations. Mid-side microphones were used to give a wider audio image.

Slide 4: Studio Work

I had several roles to achieve this goal, After the recording was completed, my first job was to upgrade the Apple computer systems in the studio, this involved replacing hardware inside the machines, firstly upgrading the SSDs, once these were installed, it turned out in order to upgrade the OS, metal-enabled GPU’s were required, which was required to process surround sound audio. This paved the way to make the project possible.

Once the computer systems were up to spec, we needed to take field recordings. We went out to a nearby waterfall. We used equipment from the studio including hydrophones, as well as some of my own recording equipment. I had never had experience using hydrophones, so this was a great learning experience. The first day we went out we discovered there was some wind noise which entered the microphones thus spoiling the recordings. Therefore, we needed to go out a second time and re-record the content.

Slide 5: Communication

I attended meetings within the company, this gave me an insight into how to conduct myself positively and professionally as well as giving me the confidence to put across constructive points in order to enhance the outcome of the project. Meetings were undertaken both online and in person. Online meeting etiquette was quite different from meeting face to face. For example, if you are in a face-to-face meeting with 6 people, you can confer with the person sat next to you in order to check the validity of an idea before presenting it within the main meeting. This is not possible with an online meeting.

During the meetings, Drew and I had lengthy discussions about safety considerations, expected weather, transport and access requirements, as well as discussing safe water levels due to rainfall or other weather conditions. We also discussed an exit strategy if anything was to go wrong. Leaving notification with third party should communication drop off whilst in remote locations. For safety reasons we agreed to never work alone and always be in sight of each other whilst on location.

Also, during my time in the studio, several clients were in, taking part in various projects. I was involved in technical discussions in various group meetings. This was an insight into how to conduct myself in a professional environment.
